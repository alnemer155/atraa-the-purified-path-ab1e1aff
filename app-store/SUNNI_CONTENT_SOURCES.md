# Sunni Content — Source Provenance

This document is provided to App Store reviewers and to any user who wants to
audit the religious content shipped to Sunni users in عِتَرَةً (Atraa).

Every dua, dhikr, and adhkar entry in `src/data/sunni-content.ts` ships with
the source / hadith reference printed directly under its title in the app.
The reference set used for compilation:

## Primary hadith collections (Kutub al-Sittah + Musnad Ahmad)
1. **Sahih al-Bukhari** — Imam Muhammad ibn Isma'il al-Bukhari (d. 256 AH)
2. **Sahih Muslim** — Imam Muslim ibn al-Hajjaj (d. 261 AH)
3. **Sunan Abi Dawud** — Imam Abu Dawud as-Sijistani (d. 275 AH)
4. **Jami' at-Tirmidhi** — Imam Muhammad ibn 'Isa at-Tirmidhi (d. 279 AH)
5. **Sunan al-Nasa'i** — Imam Ahmad ibn Shu'ayb al-Nasa'i (d. 303 AH)
6. **Sunan Ibn Majah** — Imam Muhammad ibn Yazid Ibn Majah (d. 273 AH)
7. **Musnad Ahmad** — Imam Ahmad ibn Hanbal (d. 241 AH)

## Compilation references
- **Hisn al-Muslim min Adhkar al-Kitab wa-l-Sunnah** — Sa'id bin 'Ali bin Wahf al-Qahtani.
- **al-Adhkar** — Imam Yahya ibn Sharaf al-Nawawi (d. 676 AH).
- **Riyad as-Salihin** — Imam al-Nawawi.
- **'Amal al-Yawm wa-l-Layla** — al-Nasa'i.

## Authentication notes
- Wherever an authentication grade is given (e.g. "صحّحه الألباني" /
  "حسّنه الألباني"), it refers to the modern hadith judgments of Sheikh
  **Muhammad Nasir al-Din al-Albani** (d. 1420 AH) in his works on Abu Dawud,
  al-Tirmidhi, Ibn Majah, and al-Nasa'i — widely accepted in mainstream Sunni
  scholarship today.
- Quranic verses are cited by Sura name + ayah number from the Madinah Mushaf
  (Hafs 'an 'Asim recitation), which is the dominant printed text in Sunni
  use worldwide.
